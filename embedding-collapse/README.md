# Embedding Collapse Simulator

The Embedding Collapse Simulator models the breakdown of semantic structure in high-dimensional representation spaces. It treats logical operators—modal, epistemic, agentive, and indexical—not as symbols in a formal system but as spatially distributed fields that evolve under pressure.

Each cell in the grid represents a local region of embedding space. Within each region, operators such as necessity, possibility, knowledge, belief, action, and event coexist and interact. Under ideal conditions, these operators remain separated, preserving semantic distinctions. Under pressure, they begin to converge, blur, and ultimately collapse into indistinguishable content.

The system is not a logic engine. It is a dynamical field model of semantic degradation.


---

Conceptual Background

This simulator is inspired by the idea that embedding spaces encode meaning through geometric proximity, but do not inherently preserve logical structure. When optimization pressures, noise, or compression effects dominate, semantically distinct operators may collapse into overlapping regions.

This phenomenon is referred to here as semantic collapse, following:

In this view, meaning is not lost abruptly but undergoes a gradual entropic drift in which neighborhood structure overrides formal distinctions.


---

Core Idea

Modern embedding systems rely on geometric proximity as a proxy for semantic similarity. This simulator explores the failure mode where proximity overrides structure.

Collapse occurs when:

necessity becomes indistinguishable from possibility

action becomes indistinguishable from event

knowledge becomes indistinguishable from fact


At extreme collapse, operators disappear entirely, leaving only flattened content.

This is modeled as a continuous field process rather than a discrete logical failure.


---

Field Structure

Each cell contains continuous values for:

Modal layer:

necessity (□P)

possibility (◇P)

fact (P)


Epistemic layer:

knowledge (KₐP)

belief (BₐP)


Agency layer:

action (stit)

event (evt)


Indexical layer:

self (I)

here (context anchor)


Global fields:

collapse (σ): pressure toward equivalence

mpk (κ): constraint enforcement field


These fields interact locally but produce global structure.


---

Dynamics

The system evolves in discrete time steps through coupled field updates.

Collapse pressure increases when operators that should remain distinct co-locate within the same region. As separation decreases, collapse accelerates.

Three regimes emerge:

Blurring
Operators drift toward each other but remain partially distinct.

Conflation
Operators become interchangeable; logical distinctions fail.

Type erasure
Operators collapse into content; structure disappears entirely.

Collapse spreads spatially, producing moving regions of semantic instability.


---

MPK (Modal Proofing Kernel)

The Modal Proofing Kernel (MPK) is a constraint field that enforces structural separation.

When active, it:

maintains distance between modal operators

enforces distinction between action and event

stabilizes indexical anchors

caps collapse in extreme regions


The MPK can be interpreted as a geometric analogue of logical well-formedness constraints or type systems.

Metrics

The system tracks several global quantities:

Collapse pressure
Mean collapse across the grid

Modal separation
Distinction between necessity and possibility

Agency distinction
Distinction between action and event

Epistemic clarity
Difference between knowledge and belief

Semantic entropy
Degree of operator mixing

Fidelity
Composite measure of structural preservation

These metrics describe geometric structure rather than symbolic correctness.


---

Interpretation

The simulator demonstrates that embedding-based representations can undergo structural degradation even when local similarity remains meaningful.

It suggests that:

semantic structure is not preserved automatically in geometric representations

proximity can override logical type distinctions

collapse emerges gradually but can become irreversible

constraint mechanisms are required to maintain structure


The system may be interpreted as a critique of unconstrained embedding models or as a prototype for constraint-aware semantic architectures.
